Definition¶

Apply repeated random sampling to obtain numerical results for difficult or otherwise Impossible problems

General approach:

  1. Define a domain of possible inputs
  2. Generate inputs randomly from a probability distribution over the domain
  3. Perform a deterministic computation on the inputs
  4. Aggregate (e.g. average) the results

Approximating the value of π.¶

Monte Carlo method applied to approximating the value of π.

Consider a quadrant (circular sector) inscribed in a unit square. Given that the ratio of their areas is $ \pi / 4$ , the value of π can be approximated using a Monte Carlo method.

def pi_MC(N):
    circle = 0
    X, Y = ([], [])
    R = [] 
    for i in range(N):
        x, y = (random(), random())
        X.append(x)
        Y.append(y)
        r = sqrt(x**2 +  y**2) 
        R.append(r < 1 )
        if r <1: circle += 1 
    plt.scatter(X,Y, c = R, s = 5, cmap="bwr")
    print("pi value: {}".format( circle/N * 4 ))
    return()

Monte Carlo - Reinforcemnt Learning¶

Monte Carlo methods require only experience—sample

sequences of states, actions, and rewards from actual or simulated interaction with an environment.

Monte Carlo reinforcement learning learns from episodes of experience:

  1. Empirical Risk minimization
  2. Model Free (No knowledge of MDP transitions/rewards)
  3. Learns from complete episodes (A complete game until reaching goal)
  4. One simple idea: the value function = the empirical mean Return

Example of an Episode

Monte Carlo Prediction:¶

We sample episodes from experience following policy $\pi$

$$ S_0, A_0, R_1, S_1, A_1,...,S_{T-1}, A_{T-1}, R_T \sim \pi$$

Loop for each step of episode, $t = T -1, T-2, ..., 0$

$$ G \leftarrow \gamma G + R_{t+1}$$

In other words:

$$ G = \gamma^{T-1} R _{T} +...+ \gamma R_{2} + R_{1} $$

Value function as the expected return :

$$ V_{\pi}(s) = E_{\pi}[G_t | S_t = s]$$

We usally use an empirical mean instead of the expected return.

Incremental means¶

Reinforcemt alforithms use something calles incremental means, where $\mu_1, \mu_2,...$ from a sequence

$$ \mu_k = \dfrac{1}{k} \sum_{j=1}^{k} x_j $$$$ = \dfrac{1}{k} ( x_k + \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} x_j )$$$$ = \dfrac{1}{k} ( x_k + (k-1) \mu_k -1 )$$$$ = \mu_{k-1} + \dfrac{1}{k}(x_k - \mu_{k-1}) $$

All these together: sample episodes from experience following policy $\pi$

$$ S_0, A_0, R_1, S_1, A_1,...,S_{T-1}, A_{T-1}, R_T \sim \pi$$

every time we visit a state, we’re going to increase a visit counter, then we will use our running mean:

$$ N(S_t) \leftarrow N(S_t) + 1 $$$$ V(S_t) \leftarrow V(S_t) + \dfrac{1}{N(S_t)} (G_t - V(St)) $$

ϵ -greedy exploration¶

ϵ -greedy exploration¶

The simplest idea to avoid local minima is:

  • choose a random action with probability $\epsilon$
  • choose the action greedily with probability $1-\epsilon$
  • where all m actions are tied with non-zero probability

So we have the updated policy:

$$ \pi(\alpha | s) = \begin{cases} \epsilon/m + 1 - \epsilon & if a^* = max_{a\in A} Q(s,a) \\ \epsilon/m & otherwise \end{cases} $$

Proof of converge in 5.1 of Reference [1]

limit with infinite exploration (GLIE)¶

Defines a schedule for exploration, such that these two conditions are met:

  1. Continue to explore everything
$$ \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} N_k (s,a) = \infty$$
  1. The policy converges on a greedy policy
$$ \lim_{k \rightarrow \infty} \pi_k (a|s) = 1 (a = argmax Q_k(s,a')) $$

Algorithm: greedy at the limit of ∞ exploration

for episode in range(num_episodes):
    s = env.reset(), done = False, result_list = []
    while not done:
        → e = min(1.0, 10000.0/(episode+1))
        if np.random.rand() > e:
            a = np.argmax(Q[s, :])
        else:
            a = env.action_space.sample()
    s’, reward, done, _ = env.step(a)
    results_list.append((s, a))
    result_sum += reward
    s = s’
    for (s, a) in results_list:
        n_visits[s, a] += 1.0
        α = 1.0 / n_visits[s, a]
        Q[s, a] += α ∗ (result_sum − Q[s, a])

Summary¶

In summary, Monte Carlo RL methods:

  • Solution to RF problem
  • Require training with complete episodes
  • Model free
  • Balance in Exploration vs Exploitation
  • Eventually converge on optimal policy
